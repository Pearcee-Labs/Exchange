The Möbius Strip in Architecture


The Möbius strip is one of the most remarkable structures known to man. It is a wonderful one sided surface, discovered by a German mathematician and astronomer in 1858: August Ferdinand Möbius. In fact, it’s so incredible, the Bjarke Injels Group (BIG) have constructed a magnificent National Library in Astana, Kazakhstan, which forms the shape of the Möbius strip. This striking structure is a flowing twist of metal and glass; and ingeniously, BIG has created a geometric pattern or “ecological ornament” to regulate the solar impact.

The most significant aspect of this architectural phenomenon is that its interior is its exterior and its walls are its ceilings. The building is designed to mirror the outstanding natural landscape, and to capture Astana’s sunlight. The building reflects today what mankind can create, and will leave a mathematical architectural imprint in history.

It’s exquisite how the Möbius strip can inspire us to accomplish such masterpieces as the National Library in Astana. It is forever unravelling incredible mathematical questions, and will forever inspire technology.
