The Möbius Strip in Recycling


Without recycling and care for the environment, sadly our world would be no more. Recycling is the process of turning one object that is not used to something that it useful and valuable. Recycling can be used in every part of our lives; clothes, cereal packets and even old baked bean tins can create something necessary and useful.

An example of this is the Tamar Bridge. It is a bridge between Plymouth and Cornwall and is vital to commuters every day. However this is no ordinary bridge, it is made using recycled materials such as concrete and even milk bottles! I find it amazing that our technology allows us to create something so useful and impressive using old milk bottles.

However, many people still do not recycle. For example only 17% of English rubbish in 2004 was collected for recycling and 50% of the rubbish in the average dustbin could be composted. The rubbish that is not recycled will end up in landfill; useless, wasting space and harming the environment. If the public recycled more, more energy could be saved. For example, one recycled tin can contain enough energy to power a television for three hours.

The Strauss family live in Gloucester, England and only throw away one small bin bag of rubbish each year! They do this through a process of strict recycling and composting and do not waste anything. Compare this to the rubbish of the average American, which is 726 kilograms. If we followed the example of the Strauss family, landfill, the environment and global warming would surely not be such an issue. Recycling is re-using and is a continuous cycle. This so aptly fits with the Möbius strip a never ending and continuous shape that represents a fluent cycle. We need to embody this in our recycling and make a real effort to reuse and recycle as much as possible and it is possible.
