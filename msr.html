The Möbius Strip in Religion

Buddhism

The Möbius Strip has long been a key symbol of Buddhism and its concepts; the one infinite side represents traditional Buddhist elements such as reincarnation and the teachings of the Sutras (written texts considered to be the word of the Buddha), particularly the famous Lotus Sutra and its description of the Buddha being an eternal entity who chose to remain in the cycle of samsara (rebirth) despite having achieved nirvana (enlightenment) in order to pass on his wisdom to future generations. A contemporary Buddhist temple is currently being planned by Miliy Design, an architecture firm in Shanghai, in order to promote the Buddhist way of life in China. Although it still requires approval from Buddhist authorities and a great deal of funding, the project has created much interest in both the Buddhist and architectural communities. Designed to incorporate the fundamental features of the religion into its appearance, the Möbius strip has been utilised to twist the building into a stupa (a shape similar to a pagoda). Internal features include a central spire for Buddhists to congregate at and even, according to the head architect Wang Qing, a lack of “a clear distinction between ceiling, wall, and floor” that would enable worshippers to feel the significance of reincarnation in the world around them.

The Möbius Strip as religious jewellery

Particularly in Christianity, an increasingly popular piece of religious jewellery is the Mobius ring; the continuous loop can be interpreted as a representation of proof that God is the creator of the universe as he has “always been”, with neither a beginning nor an end to his existence. Similarly, when the Mobius strip is cut in two along the centre, the link created is taken to signify the illusion of separateness from God despite having come from the same source and even as evidence for the soul since the inner edge (“the soul”) is indistinguishable from the outer edge (“the body”). Similarly, in Sikhism the kara – one of the five kakars, which are external symbolisms of the faith of the wearer – is a steel or iron bracelet that is worn on the right wrist of the believer. This can be twisted into a Möbius strip as a symbol of God’s qualities, which include akaal (timelessness or eternal existence) and his infinite power, and also as an icon of reincarnation similar to that of Buddhism.
