John Amos Comenius

“We are all citizens of one world.”


The Comenius Programme was named after Jon Amos Comenius (1592-1670), who was a 17th century Czech teacher, educator, philosopher and writer. Nowadays, Jon Amos Comenius is often regarded as the “father of modern education”.

The Comenius programme is a European Union educational project and is part of the EU’s “Lifelong Learning Programme” (2007-2013). The programme aims to unite schools, colleges and local authorities across Europe to develop students’ knowledge and understanding of the diversity of European cultures and languages, as well as help young people acquire basic life skills for their personal development and future employment as well as become active European citizens.

The programmes aim to develop students’ learning motivation and the key skill of Language learning while also being centralised around students’ creativity and innovation. The programmes also aim to improve students’ scientific knowledge and improve their awareness of greater literacy and entrepreneurship across Europe.

The ultimate goal of the programme is to have at least 3 million students and teachers participating in international activities throughout the European Union by the end of 2013.
